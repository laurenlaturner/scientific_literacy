At the end of March 2025, I met with a biology instructor who had started on a research project. 
With education at the core of her questions, I was chosen to be the undergraduate statistician to take on this task.
After completing the necessary trainings to comply with regulation standards, this instructor and I sat together to
sift through paper consent forms, deleting from our digital data all those that had not given consent. Then came the
grueling process of cleaning, organizing, and deidentifying the data.

With clean data, it was decided that two-sided t-tests (for numerical, TOSLS score data) and various forms of correlation tests (for
ordinal, Likert-scale data) would be used to determine if significant differences existed within pre and post assessments.

To determine if a significant relationship existed between TOSLS assessment scores and Likert-scale self assessment averages,
two regression models were constructed, analyzed, and compared. 

Various conclusions were thus drawn. A more complete explanation of the findings of this study are included in the Jupyter Notebook
within this repository.
